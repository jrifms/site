📘 Modos, Tempos e Verbais em Ação

Foco nas palavras: Have, Cold, Might, Gotten, Do, Will, Would — com dezenas de exemplos e exercícios com correção interativa.

1. HAVE / HAS + GOTTEN

Uso: Indica ações no passado com relevância no presente (Present Perfect) ou que se tornaram algo. Gotten é o particípio passado de get (comum nos EUA). No Reino Unido, usa-se mais got.

  • I have gotten a terrible cold. Consequência agora
  • She has gotten much better at English. Tornou-se
  • We have gotten all the information we need. Obtivemos
  • Have you gotten the results yet?
  • He has gotten cold feet about the wedding. Expressão: ficou com medo
  • By the time we arrived, they had already gotten there. Past Perfect

2. MIGHT (Possibilidade)

Uso: Possibilidade no presente/futuro (menos certeza que may) ou passado com might have + gotten.

  • It might get cold tonight, bring a jacket.
  • She might have gotten lost on the way here.
  • I might not have gotten the job, but I tried.
  • If you don’t take medicine, your cold might get worse.
  • They might do the presentation tomorrow.
  • He might have gotten a promotion, but he didn't tell anyone.

3. WILL (Futuro e Certeza)

Uso: Futuro certo, promessas, decisões espontâneas e previsões baseadas em opinião.

  • I will have a cup of tea for my cold. Decisão
  • It will be cold tomorrow, wear a coat. Previsão
  • Don't worry, you will get better soon. Promessa
  • Will you do me a favor? Pedido
  • By next winter, I will have gotten used to the cold. Futuro Perfeito
  • If you take this syrup, your cough will go away. 1ª Condicional

4. WOULD (Condicional, Hábito e Cortesia)

Uso: Hipóteses (2ª e 3ª Condicionais), ações repetidas no passado e pedidos educados.

  • If I had a cold, I would stay home. 2ª Condicional
  • I would have gotten the vaccine, but I was traveling. 3ª Condicional
  • Would you mind if I closed the window? Pedido educado
  • When I was a kid, I would get a cold every winter. Hábito passado
  • If I were you, I would do the test again. Conselho
  • She said she would have gotten here earlier, but the train was late. Discurso indireto

5. DO (Auxiliar e Verbo Principal)

Uso: Perguntas, negativas, ênfase e como verbo fazer.

  • Do you have a cold? Pergunta
  • I do not (don't) think it will be cold today. Negação
  • I do want to go, even with a cold. Ênfase
  • What do you usually do when you get a cold? Aux + Verbo
  • She doesn't have the keys; she might have gotten them stolen.
  • Did you get the cold from your brother? Passado

6. COLD (Adjetivo e Substantivo)

Uso: Pode significar frio (temperatura) ou resfriado (doença).

  • Presente: I have a cold, so I feel cold all the time.
  • Passado: It was so cold yesterday that I wore two sweaters.
  • Futuro: It will be very cold in New York next week.
  • Condicional: If it gets cold, I would turn on the heater.
  • Hipótese passada: I might have gotten sick because of the cold weather.
  • Com "do": Do you feel cold, or is it just me?

📝 Exercício 1: Complete com a palavra correta

Palavras disponíveis: have, has, might, will, would, do, did, gotten

  1. If she ___________ studied harder, she ___________ have passed the exam.
  2. I ___________ never ___________ such a terrible cold in my life!
  3. ___________ you think it ___________ rain tomorrow? I hope not.
  4. He ___________ not want to go to the hospital, but he ___________ have to if the fever continues.
  5. Where ___________ you ___________ that information? I need it now!
  6. If I were you, I ___________ take a week off to recover from that cold.
  7. She ___________ have arrived by now, but her flight was canceled.

Correção Comentada

1. had / would — (3ª Condicional: arrependimento).
2. have / gotten — (Present Perfect: experiência de vida).
3. Do / will — (Pergunta no presente + futuro).
4. does / will — (Negativa no presente + futuro).
5. did / get — (Atenção! Com "did", o verbo volta ao infinitivo: GET).
6. would — (2ª Condicional: conselho).
7. would (ou might) — (Ela teria chegado, mas o voo cancelou).

📝 Exercício 2: Múltipla Escolha

  1. "I'm feeling terrible." - "You ___________ have gotten the flu."
    a) will   b) might   c) do   d) would
  2. If it ___________ so cold yesterday, we ___________ gone to the beach.
    a) hadn't been / would have   b) isn't / will   c) wasn't / would   d) hasn't been / might
  3. He ___________ his homework yet, but he promises he ___________ do it later.
    a) haven't done / will   b) hasn't gotten / do   c) hasn't done / will   d) didn't do / would
  4. When I lived in Canada, I ___________ often get sick during the winter.
    a) would   b) will   c) might   d) have
  5. We ___________ need to bring extra blankets because the hotel ___________ not have heating.
    a) might / do   b) will / might   c) would / has   d) do / will

Correção Comentada

1. b) might — Possibilidade (Talvez tenha pego a gripe).
2. a) hadn't been / would have — 3ª Condicional.
3. c) hasn't done / will — Present Perfect negativa + promessa futura.
4. a) would — Hábito no passado.
5. b) will / might — Certeza (vamos precisar) + possibilidade (talvez não tenha).

📝 Exercício 3: Reescreva as frases

  1. Perhaps she caught a cold at the party. (use: MIGHT HAVE GOTTEN)
  2. I promise to call you when I arrive. (use: WILL)
  3. He refused to do the exercise because he was tired. (use: WOULDN'T)
  4. I really think you should take this seriously. (use: DO para ênfase)
  5. It was possible that the weather turned cold, but I didn't check. (use: MIGHT HAVE GOTTEN)

Correção Comentada

1. She might have gotten a cold at the party.
2. I will call you when I arrive.
3. He wouldn't do the exercise because he was tired.
4. I do think you should take this seriously.
5. The weather might have gotten cold, but I didn't check.

📝 Exercício 4: Corrija os erros

  1. I have get a cold last week.
  2. If I will have a cold, I would stay home.
  3. She might got the job, but she decided not to take it.
  4. Do you would like some hot soup?
  5. He doesn't have got any money.

Correção Comentada

1. I got a cold last week. (Passado definido last week exige Simple Past, não Present Perfect).
2. If I have a cold, I will stay home. (1ª Condicional - real) ou If I had a cold, I would stay home. (2ª Condicional - hipótese). Em condicionais, não usamos will na parte do "if".
3. She might have gotten the job, but she decided not to take it. (Depois de might usamos infinitivo perfeito: have gotten).
4. Would you like some hot soup? (Não se junta do com would em perguntas. Would já é o auxiliar).
5. He hasn't got any money. (Britânico) ou He doesn't have any money. (Americano/Correto). A estrutura doesn't have got está incorreta.

🚨 Dicas de Ouro para Não Errar

Os 6 pecados capitais da gramática com have, cold, might, gotten, do, will, would — e como evitá-los para sempre!

1️⃣ HAVE GOT x HAVE GOTTEN

❌ Erro: "I have gotten a car" (para dizer que tem um carro).
✅ Certo: I have got a car (posse) / I have gotten a car (consegui/comprei).
💡 Macete: Tenho = HAVE GOT. Peguei/Me tornei = HAVE GOTTEN.

2️⃣ NUNCA use WILL com IF

❌ Erro: "If I will have a cold, I will stay."
✅ Certo: If I have a cold, I will stay. (Presente + Futuro)
💡 Macete: Na parte do "IF" o verbo fica no PRESENTE (para futuro real) ou no PASSADO (para hipótese). Will e Would ficam na OUTRA parte da frase.

3️⃣ MIGHT no passado = MIGHT HAVE GOTTEN

❌ Erro: "She might got the job."
✅ Certo: She might have gotten the job.
💡 Macete: Decore o pacote: MIGHT + HAVE + PARTICÍPIO (GOTTEN). É um trio inseparável para o passado!

4️⃣ DO e WOULD não se misturam

❌ Erro: "Do you would like some tea?"
✅ Certo: Would you like some tea? (oferecimento) ou Do you like tea? (gosto).
💡 Macete: Educado/Hipotético = WOULD sozinho. Fato/Presente = DO sozinho. Escolha UM!

5️⃣ CUIDADO com o "COLD" (doença x temperatura)

❌ Erro: "I have cold" (sem o 'a') ou "I am a cold".
✅ Certo: I have a cold (doença) / I am cold (sentindo frio).
💡 Macete: Resfriado = tem "A" (um). Temperatura = sem "A".

6️⃣ Datas específicas exigem PASSADO SIMPLES (GOT)

❌ Erro: "I have gotten a cold last week."
✅ Certo: I got a cold last week. (passado definido)
💡 Macete: Yesterday, last week, in 2010 = GOT. Ever, never, yet, already = HAVE GOTTEN.

🎯 BÔNUS: WOULD para hábitos no passado

Se você quer dizer que costumava fazer algo repetidamente no passado, pode usar WOULD. Ex: "When I was a kid, I would get a cold every winter." (Mas cuidado: se for um estado, tipo "ser alto", use ONLY "used to").
